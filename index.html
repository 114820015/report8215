AI Revolution
Evolved: Gemini 3

The Action Agent Era

Redefining the boundaries of EECS engineering

I. The Evolution

The evolution of AI is truly moving too fast. Looking back over the past two years, I viewed tools like ChatGPT merely as knowledgeable librarians—great for looking up information and summarizing text. But the release of Gemini 3 is a huge turning point for me. It is no longer just a "Chatbot" for conversation; it is an "Action Agent" capable of actually building products.

II. Execution Speed
Gemini 3 shatters the "implementation barrier" that once felt so distant. Previously, developing a web game was a weeks-long battle for me, requiring me to "grind out" lines of code one by one. Now, it has become an automated process taking mere minutes.

"I typed: 'Make an interactive Christmas gift-catching game.' In less than three minutes, Gemini 3 generated a logically correct, fully playable web game."

This capability even extends to 3D. The "Zero Threshold" era has truly arrived; realizing creativity is no longer stuck in tedious technical details.
III. Structural Threat

As an EECS student, seeing this speed actually sends a chill down my spine. In Taiwan, a junior web designer earns about 38k to 47k TWD, and a game designer starts around 34k to 42k TWD. These salaries are what the market originally paid for our ability to write code and fix bugs—basic skills I spent years mastering.

But the reality is, if AI can write cleaner, more efficient code in seconds than I—a junior engineer—can in days, the value of an "entry-level engineer" collapses instantly. Future companies might only need senior architects paired with AI tools, bypassing the need for junior staff like us entirely.

IV. Survival Strategy
The conclusion is clear. If I am just someone who knows how to write code, I will be waiting to be replaced. So, I must evolve from a "Coder" into a "Problem Solver."

1 Strategic Thinking

Understanding "what to build" and "why," not just "how."

2 Architecture

Learning how to assemble different modules into a stable system.

3 Creativity

Focusing on user experience and emotional connections.

Gemini 3 marks the shift of AI from "conversation" to "implementation." This "Zero Threshold" high-speed output is the most direct challenge for us EECS students still in school. If AI can instantly replicate the work of a junior engineer, my coding skills are no longer a guaranteed safety net, and the risk of displacement is real.

Therefore, transformation is imperative. My future core competitiveness cannot stop at "learning how to write code"; instead, I must possess "Imagination," "Problem-Solving Skills," and "Strategic Thinking."

In conclusion, this doesn't mean my engineering career ends before it begins; rather, it forces me to "upgrade" now. My role moves from executor to core coordinator. Success depends on imagination to define problems and the strategy to guide AI. This is the path our generation of engineers must take.
