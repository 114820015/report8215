AI Revolution
Evolved: Gemini 3

The Action Agent Era

Redefining the boundaries of EECS engineering

I. The Evolution

AI has evolved. In the past two years, I viewed tools like ChatGPT as knowledgeable librarians—great for text and summaries. However, the release of Gemini 3 marks a pivotal shift for me: AI is no longer just a "Chatbot"; it is an "Action Agent" capable of building real products.

II. Execution Speed
Gemini 3 shatters the barrier between idea and execution. Traditionally, building a digital experience was a weeks-long relay race for me, requiring lines of code written one by one. Now, it is an automated process taking mere minutes.

"I simply type: 'Create a mini-game for Christmas where users catch falling presents and avoid coal.' In three minutes, Gemini 3 generates a fully playable, interactive game with animations."

This capability extends to 3D and physics-based logic, allowing me to bring festive ideas to life with a single prompt. The "Zero Threshold" era has arrived.
III. Structural Threat

As an EECS student, this speed brings a chill. In Taiwan, a junior web designer earns between 38,000 and 47,000 TWD per month, while game designers start around 34,000 to 42,000 TWD. These salaries are paid for the ability to write code and fix bugs—skills I spent years mastering.

However, if AI can write cleaner code in seconds than I—a junior engineer—can in days, the value of "entry-level coding" collapses. Companies may simply pair senior architects with AI tools, bypassing the need for junior staff entirely.

IV. Survival Strategy
The conclusion is clear: If I only write code, I will be replaced. To survive, I must evolve from a "Coder" to a "Problem Solver."

1 Strategic Thinking

Know what to build and why.

2 Architecture

Assembling modules into stable systems.

3 Creativity

Defining the user experience and emotion.

In conclusion, this does not end my career but facilitates my professional upgrade. My role moves from basic execution to core coordination. My future success depends on my ability to guide AI to build systems and solve actual problems, rather than relying solely on manual coding techniques.
